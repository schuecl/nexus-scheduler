# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Nexus Scheduler is built and maintained in the open, and we want
participating in it — filing issues, reviewing pull requests,
discussing designs, asking questions — to be a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or
invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity
and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

We are a small project; a healthy, professional tone is what keeps it
sustainable.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive. Critique code and ideas, not people.
- Assume good faith. Many contributors work on this in limited time and
  across time zones; give reviews and replies the benefit of the doubt.
- Keep feedback specific and actionable — "this query can miss the
  index" is useful; "this is bad" is not.
- Accept that maintainers may decline a change. Disagreement is fine;
  relitigating a settled decision across multiple threads is not.
- Respect the security policy: report vulnerabilities privately per
  [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md), never in a public issue.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments, including in
  commit messages, code comments, and review threads.
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or sustained bad-faith argument.
- Publishing others' private information (physical or email address,
  employer, etc.) without their explicit permission.
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome advances of any kind.
- Any other conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — the repository
(issues, pull requests, discussions, commit history), and anywhere an
individual is representing the project in public.

## Enforcement

Violations can be reported privately to the project maintainers —
through a GitHub private message, or by the private contact route
described in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) if you prefer not to use a
public channel at all. Reports will be reviewed promptly and kept as
confidential as the situation allows.

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these
standards and may take any action they deem appropriate, including
editing or removing comments, commits, issues, and pull requests that
violate this Code of Conduct, and temporarily or permanently banning a
contributor for behavior that is inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful. Maintainers who fail to uphold these standards may be
removed from the project.
